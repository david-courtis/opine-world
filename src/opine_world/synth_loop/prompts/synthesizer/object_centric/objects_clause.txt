OBJECTS INFLUENCE ONE ANOTHER, AND THE INFLUENCE IS GUARDED BY PRECONDITIONS. The transition_function's job is to encode the OBJECT-LEVEL RELATIONSHIPS that fire in response to an action: 'object A pushes object B only when condition C on world state holds.' The interactions are rarely one-object-in-isolation. The co-variation of objects across transitions in the replay buffer is your primary evidence. when object X changes, what else changes with it? Under what state did that co-variation occur, and when did it NOT occur even though X was acted on?
